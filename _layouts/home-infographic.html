Description

This resource is designed to facilitate the inclusion of Palestinian voices, sources, stories, and themes across a broad range of university courses. It catalogs materials in ways intended for use in teaching while featuring thematic collections and clusters that are easily slotted into classroom teaching on any number of topics. 

Purpose of the Website

This is a project of imagining and laboring towards a Palestinian Studies shaped in and through collaborative networks of research and pedagogy. Working alongside rather than in place of the crucial task of creating new faculty lines in Palestinian Studies, we aim to organically propagate Palestinian Studies across a range of scholarly fields and levels of expertise through the sharing and curating of pedagogical resources. Our task, in other words, is to develop both a product - an open-access digital resource - and a novel kind of process that diffuses the labor of Palestinian Studies among scholars with varying levels and kinds of expertise.

How to navigate through the website?

You can browse the resources and additional materials in a variety of ways, depending on your interests and research needs. The site aims to support both guided exploration and targeted searching. Currently, you can explore: Collections, Pedagogical Clusters, and Special Projects; and you can use the timeline and filter tools to narrow your search.

Collection: [description; how you might use it]. Introduction videos are linked for each collection, and you click on the collection title, which will bring you to the individual page for each collection

Pedagogical Cluster: These are small thematic “clusters” of materials. They are designed to support teaching and learning from a range of disciplinary perspectives by connecting sources on a specific topic with guiding discussion questions. You can access individual clusters through the drop-down menu bar and the right column on the home page.

Special Projects: [description; how you might use it] This section is featured on the home page. Click on the project title to access detailed materials and curated sources.

Timeline: [change after feature] The timeline is useful for understanding historical progression and connections between events, and for researching specific time periods. You can access the Timeline through the menu bar and right column on the home page. You can access sources by clicking on the article title in the timeline.

Filter: The platform offers targeted and flexible searching across all available content. Users can refine results through keywords, collections, and pedagogical clusters, enabling users to quickly locate relevant materials.
