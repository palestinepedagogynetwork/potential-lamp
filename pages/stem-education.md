# Palestinian STEM Education in the Context of Chemistry
[image]

## Statement of Pedagogical Intent
These materials invite students to consider how the potential of violence and prorated conflict can affect education in general and scientific education in particular. They focus on the disciplinary development of Chemistry in Palestine and the unique circumstances surrounding Chemistry education for Palestinian students. Presenting studies of the history of chemistry education in Palestine, along with the challenges faced in contemporary times, the sources provoke questions about how scientific knowledge is produced, circulated, and valued in a society shaped by political constraint. This cluster is meant to push against the tendency to view Palestine only through the lens of conflict, and instead highlight how classrooms, laboratories, teachers, and students nevertheless participate in building scientific literacy and local expertise. The goal is to help learners recognize the relationships that emerge when one asks what “chemistry in Palestine” actually entails. These include the relationships among education, communication, land, scientific practice, and cultural context. In addition to presenting a useful history of disciplinary formation, studying these dynamics can offer a more expansive and humanizing picture of Palestinian society.

## Discussion Questions
1.  If one were to bring up the subject of Chemistry in Palestine, what areas of focus and relationships would appear?
2.  In terms of teaching STEM in Palestine, what struggles do you think occur?
