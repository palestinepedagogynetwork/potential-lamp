# Palestinian Trauma
[image]

## Statement of Pedagogical Intent
This cluster focuses on how assessments of trauma in Palestine are conveyed through both numbers and lived experiences. This grouping of materials aims to help students come to see trauma not only as an individual psychological condition, but as a collective and structural experience shaped by environment, violence, and instability. These sources will guide students to contemplate how the concept of PTSD (post-traumatic stress disorder) may be inadequate or even misleading in a context where violence is ongoing.
Sources include qualitative medical case studies that analyze trauma in Palestine, statistical analyses, and observational research that depicts health numerically. Using these materials, students will come to deeply understand trauma not as a discrete psychological event followed by recovery, but as a persistent condition shaped by continuous exposure to violence and uncertainty. Engagement with the readings will highlight to students how trauma can be everyday and how traditional diagnostic frameworks may fail to fully capture these experiences.


## Discussion Questions
1.  What assumptions does the diagnosis of PTSD make about time and recovery? How does the author challenge those assumptions in the context of Palestine?  
2.  How does the second reading on the concept of "humiliation" expand our understanding of trauma beyond traditional definitions like PTSD?
