# Queer Palestinians & Pinkwashing
[image]

## Statement of Pedagogical Intent
The goal of this pedagogical cluster is to spark a conversation about pinkwashing as Israeli propaganda and how a conventionally Western audience may approach queer Palestinians and queer resistance in Palestine. The intention behind pairing these materials together is to provoke questions of how a settler-colonial narrative is constructed, by whom, and for what purpose. How might we deconstruct this settler-colonial narrative and titrate out the individual working parts before reassembling a decolonial narrative? These materials will lead students to better understanding the mechanisms of state-supervised propaganda and how its narrative is crafted over time and throughout occupation, extending to how identities are constructed on both individual and national levels. Pinkwashing operates as propaganda by exploiting queer people and projecting a facade of civility while concealing abuses of power and militaristic occupation of Palestinian land. The conceptual implication of pinkwashing is the erasure of queer people that are oppressed by the Israeli state, allowing the Israeli state to position themselves as a progressive and a valiant defender of LGBTQ+ rights while actively oppressing queer people in the land they occupy. Students are invited to examine how the Israeli state is pinkwashing their defense force (IDF) to give the illusion of safety for LGBTQ+ people globally and assert their dominance as a militaristic state.


## Discussion Questions
1. How does pinkwashing operate as a tool of settler-colonial violence in Palestine? In other countries globally?
2. How does the narrative of Israel being a “liberal, accepting democracy” and Palestine as “homophobic” obscure the perception of violence within occupation?
3. How can we look to queer Palestinian lived experience to shape our own understanding of what it means to resist or create change?

## In-class activity
Alternative advertisement group project: assign students into groups (or let them choose their groups) of 3 - 5 students and provide to each group an advertisement that is engaged in pinkwashing (see images below). Have students identify the instances of pinkwashing and details ways in which they could redesign the marketing to make it authentic.
